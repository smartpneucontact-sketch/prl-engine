Upload your policy documents here — CBA articles, HRPM sections, FAA Orders, management guides, leadership memoranda, local procedures, and technical bulletins. PRL will chunk, index, and store them for instant semantic retrieval when you ask a policy question. The more comprehensive your knowledge base, the more accurate and defensible PRL's guidance becomes.

Operational Schedule

Track upcoming operational events, equipment reviews, stakeholder briefings, union collaboration meetings, and maintenance windows. Events are sorted by date and color-coded by priority. Use this to maintain situational awareness of deadlines that may trigger policy actions (e.g., CBA notification timelines, leave restriction periods, or budget submission dates).

Communications

Manage operational communications with built-in routing to key stakeholders: ETR Division, HR, Supervisors, AIT Leadership, PASS Union Representatives, and FAA Legal. Use the Compose function to draft governance-ready emails — PRL can help generate policy-compliant language for notifications, solicitations, and formal correspondence. Unread messages are highlighted with a gold indicator.

Letter Templates

Governance-Ready Correspondence

Pre-formatted letter templates for common management actions across disciplinary, administrative, scheduling, labor relations, recognition, and reporting categories. Each template includes the proper structure, required elements, and policy references. Templates are designed to be HR-approvable and legally defensible — built from real operational use inside FAA Technical Operations with 100% HR approval rate.

Governance Controls

Regulatory & Compliance Framework

Track the regulatory, compliance, and policy frameworks that govern PRL's operation and the decisions it supports. This includes federal mandates (OMB, FISMA, Executive Orders), agency-specific policies (FAA Orders, DOT directives), labor relations agreements (PASS CBA), HR policy (HRPM), and security authorizations (FedRAMP). Each item shows its current status and applicability to your operational environment.

Decision Audit Trail

Institutional Memory & Feedback Loop

Every question asked through PRL is logged here with its full response, sources used, and reasoning chain. This is PRL's institutional memory — the feedback loop that captures not just what policy says, but how it was applied in practice. Approve decisions that were accurate, flag those that need review, or reject incorrect guidance. Over time, this audit trail documents your organization's decision patterns, builds defensible records, and improves PRL's understanding of how policy is actually practiced in your environment.
